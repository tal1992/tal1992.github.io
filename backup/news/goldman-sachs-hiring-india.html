Goldman Sachs ups tech hiring by 25-30% in India

Oct 8, 2017

Goldman Sachs is boosting its technology base in India and analysts believe it is due to the emergence of financial technology challenges. It is also believed that it’s an attempt to increase revenue from digital finance operations after the collapse of bond trading revenue.

Goldman’s technology hiring is present at entry level and high-level positions. The co-chief operating officer at Goldman Sachs, Gunjan Samtani said that Goldman Sachs is boosting its recruits from Indian engineering colleges by 25-30 percent this year in order to make a larger push into technology. Its engineering hiring from Indian campuses has gone up from 120 in 2014 to 150 this year. The number of summer interns has also gone up from 200 in 2016 to 370 at this time.

The company visited about 27 engineering colleges last year, including 4 NITs and 8 IITs. This year, it plans to visit 7 more NITs and 9 more IITs. If that happens, the total engineering colleges it visits would be close to 50.

Samtani also stated that earlier, hiring activity was focused on areas like operations, accounting, and technology only. Now engineers are hired due to their expertise in statistics, computer science, and mathematics.

Goldman’s coding challenge, GS Quantify that engages students to participate in programming challenges is into its fifth edition now. Till now, about 8,000 students have participated in this challenge and more than 600 submissions have been done.

As a part of this popular programming challenge, teams have created solutions in modeling correlations between varied financial products, predicting bond liquidity and pricing exotic financial contracts that are used in risk management.

The latest edition of this challenge will take place this month and it will open to all engineering students, no matter which year they are in, rather than being open to final year students only, which was a trend till now.
